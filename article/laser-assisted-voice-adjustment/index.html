Laser assisted voice adjustment

Surgery to raise the fundamental frequency (pitch) of the voice by shortening the vibrating part of the vocal chords using lasers

Laser assisted voice adjustment may also be known as Laser assisted voice adjustment or Laser shortening glottoplasty.

What does it do?

  • Voice

    Raises pitch

The amount of time these effects last for is currently not well understood.

More information

⚠ Warning

This technique is a surgery with a number of risks you need to understand before it is carried out. Common side effects are a quieter voice, a voice with less vocal range, or decreased clarity. The surgery may also result in no change to the pitch of the voice or a deepening of the voice (Song & Jiang, 2017).

Speech therapy is able to allow the majority of people to raise the pitch and change the qualities of their voice.

However, in a minority of the cases, this is not the case, and surgery can be carried out, though it requires subsequent additional speech therapy afterwards (Seal, 2007).

While a variety of different approaches to increasing vocal pitch have been tried, there is currently little research into what the long term effects of these surgeries are, and how long the effects will last is unknown (Van Damme, Cosyns, Deman, Van den Eede, & Van Borsel, 2017).
