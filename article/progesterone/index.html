Progesterone

Hormone sometimes used in conjuction with oestrogen therapy and also for stopping periods

Progesterone may also be known as Progestins.

What does it do?

  • Menstruation

    Stops

  • Upper body

    (Possibly) increased breast growth

More information

Progesterone (in particular, medroxyprogesterone or norethisterone) can be used to supress menstruation (Seal, 2007). More recently, GnRH agonists have also been used for this purpose. (TODO: work out whether to cite Seal 2015 here or something else?)

Sometimes when oestrogen is used, progesterone is also used, as some people have claimed that it affects breast size or shape. There is currently no clear evidence whether this is helpful (Wierckx, Gooren, & T’sjoen, 2014). Some endocrinologists have also suggested that use of progesterone may lead to an increased risk of breast cancer (Seal, 2007).
