Oestrogen

Hormone with many effects including fat redistribution, skin thinning and breast growth

Oestrogen may also be known as Oestrogen, Estrogen, Estradiol, or 17β-estradiol.

What does it do?

  • Facial hair

    Thins

  • Upper body

    Breasts grow

  • Lower body

    Larger hips and buttocks, less erections

  • Mood

    More emotional

Some of the effects of Oestrogen (including breat growth) are permanent and start within 2-3 months.

More information

⚠ Warning

Taking oestrogen may increase the risk of thromboembolic disease (problems caused by blood clotting). This includes a higher risk of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) (Seal, 2007). Where possible, oestrogen should be taken under medical supervision with regular blood testing.

⚠ Warning

Taking oestrogen causes development of breast tissue, and all people who have taken oestrogen should be aware that they are at risk of breast cancer. There is no recommended method for breast self-examination in the UK, but you should learn how your breast tissue feels and looks and see a doctor if you experience any unexpected changes.

Oestrogen is commonly available in oral (pill) form, transdermal patches (patches that are stuck to the skin), gels applied to the skin, and as depot injections.

There are several different variants of oestrogen available: normally valerate or hemihydrate in the UK, and sometimes cypionate or bezoate are used (which have a longer lasting effect). In the past ethinylestradiol and conjugated equine oestrogens were used, but these are now avoided because they have a much higher risk of blood clots (Toorians et al., 2003) and are also not detectable in normal blood tests.

Oestrogen in high doses has in the past been used to supress testosterone but use of antiandrogens (in the UK, normally GnRH agonists) is now the standard approach for testosterone supression (Seal, 2007).

Oestrogen therapy is not usually provided to under-18s, with the effects of hormones during puberty being stopped by GnRH agonists until the age of 18 when oestrogen therapy can be commenced.
